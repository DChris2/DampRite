Three Common Types of Damp

1) Rising Damp

Moisture travels up from the ground through capillaries in masonry. Often seen as tide marks, salt deposits, and damaged skirtings.

2) Penetrating Damp

Water ingress through defects: cracked render, porous brick, leaks, failed pointing, or bridging. Can occur on any level.

3) Condensation

Humidity condenses on cold surfaces, frequently behind furniture and in corners. Look for mould and peeling paint.

Improving Ventilation & Vegetation Control

Keep air paths clear and reduce any vegetation preventing air getting to external walls. This helps moisture evaporate and reduces mould risk.

Brief History of DPC

Damp-proof courses (DPCs) became common in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in the UK, using slate, bitumen, or modern membranes to block capillary rise.
